# Real Estate Investment Takeaways

## Tax & Legal Strategies
- **1031 Exchange:** Use for reinvesting in properties. If rented for 2+ years, you have 45 days to identify a new property and 180 days to close.
- **Self-Directed IRA:** Can be used for investing in real estate; requires a management company.
- **Capital Gains:**
  - Sell < 1 year: Short-term capital gain.
  - Sell > 1 year: Long-term capital gain.
- **LLC Scaling:** Scaling indefinitely using an LLC for properties.

## Financial & Loan Strategies
- **DSCR (Debt-Service Coverage Ratio) Loan:**
  - Qualifying is based purely on rental income, not personal W-2 income.
  - Scale indefinitely as it doesn't rely on personal debt-to-income ratios.
  - Typical monthly mortgage for a DADU (Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit): ~$3,300–$3,400.
- **Interest Rates:** Duplex/Fourplex properties often have lower interest rates compared to larger commercial buildings.
- **The Speed Hack:** Adding just $3,000 extra to the principal once a year shaves 7.5 years off a 30-year term. Doing it monthly or quarterly can turn a 30-year plan into a 7–10 year plan.
- **Principal Paydown:** Take all excess monthly cash flow from your entire portfolio and throw it at the principal balance of your lowest mortgage.

## Construction & Development (ADUs/DADUs)
- **Dimensions & Costs:**
  - 1,200 sq. ft. build: ~$450k + $25k interest.
  - 1,200 sq. ft. (3B/2B) is most optimized for After Repair Value (ARV).
  - 3B/2B (1,100–1,200 sq. ft.) rents for about $3.5k–$4k per month in Seattle.
- **Timeline:** Survey + Permits (~5–6 months) + Build (~9 months) = ~15 months total.
- **Value Creation:**
  - **Condominiumization:** Creates value and can enable as little as 0% down payment for construction.
  - **"Two-Wheel Garage":** Built to satisfy occupancy codes, then converted to legal office space to maximize livable square footage.
  - **Refinancing:** Refinance the original purchase loan so 100% of debt is tied only to Lot A. Lot B (the backyard) is then owned free and clear for ADU development.
- **Unexpected Costs:** Bad soil requiring digging down 10 feet can cost an unexpected $50,000.
- **Hold Viability:** Failing to secure off-street parking limits long-term hold viability.

## Neighborhood Analysis
- **Neighborhood Classes:**
  - **B-Neighborhood (Middle Class - $800k to $1.2M):** The "Sweet Spot."
    - Tech workers and dual-income families.
    - **Pros:** Reliable tenants, 3x rent-to-income ratios, massive appreciation (Seattle B-Class historically doubles every 7-9 years).
    - **Strategy:** The ideal place to build ADUs and hold for 20 years.
  - **West Seattle:** Currently classified as a B-Zone.

## Investment Strategy: What to Buy & Avoid
- **What to Buy:**
  - Single-family homes with massive backyards and alley access (banking land for future ADU).
  - Cosmetic fixer duplex/triplex/fourplex.
- **Don't Buys (The Red Flags):**
  - Busy Streets / Intersections.
  - Flood Zones.
  - "D" Neighborhoods.
  - Historic Properties (strict codes).
  - Priced purely on Airbnb income.
  - High HOA Condos.
  - Bad Floor Plans / Ugly Street Appeal.
  - Next to a School.
  - Overbuilt homes in undervalued areas.
  - Luxury rentals.

## Common Mistakes & Specs
- **Vague Specs:** Not specifying "8-foot solid doors" and "2-inch mitered quartz" leads GCs to buy cheap materials (hollow doors/1cm quartz), killing luxury appraisals.
- **Materials:** Windows (~$6k–$8k white, $10k–$14k for other colors).
